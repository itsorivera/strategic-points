# Lesson 37 of 49 – Risks of Generative AI

Generative AI is powerful but brings several risks. Outputs can be biased, inaccurate, or harmful if not used carefully. Understanding these challenges is essential for responsible use.

## Risks

### 1. Bias & Impaired Fairness
Generative AI models can inherit biases from their training data, leading to unfair or discriminatory outputs that can damage reputations and violate regulations.

*Real‑world example*: Image generation for “nurse” returns only female nurses.

### 2. Inaccuracy (Hallucinations)
Models may produce false, misleading, or nonsensical information. Low‑quality input data or data latency can also cause inaccurate responses, eroding trust and leading to poor decisions.

*Real‑world example*: A law firm cites a fabricated legal precedent generated by AI.

### 3. Privacy
AI tools may misuse personal or sensitive data without proper consent.

*Real‑world example*: Private conversations with an AI assistant, including medical questions and passwords, were inadvertently exposed in search engine results.

### 4. Harmful Use
Generative AI can be weaponized to create fake news, offensive material, or impersonate individuals.

*Real‑world example*: A finance employee transferred funds after a deep‑fake video call impersonated senior executives.

## Managing These Risks

- **Think critically**: Evaluate inputs and outputs, request sources, and cross‑check facts.
- **Be aware of bias**: Learn about AI bias and how to detect it.
- **Know the law**: Understand local AI regulations that may affect tool usage.
- **Protect data**: Follow organizational policies on sharing personal or customer data and use only approved AI tools.
- **Verify everything**: Do not take AI output at face value. Improve digital literacy, use reverse‑image search, and other verification techniques.
