# Evaluating When to Use Generative AI

_Key Insights from McKinsey Forward Program - Lesson 25_

Generative AI is a powerful tool, but it is not a universal solution. Employing Gen AI successfully requires **human judgment** to determine when AI adds value and when traditional methods or personal expertise are superior.

---

## Decision Matrix: Task Suitability

| Suitable for Gen AI (Yes) | Unsuitable for Gen AI (No) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Exploratory & Iterative:** Brainstorming, drafts, ideas. | **Accuracy-Critical:** Fact-checking, precise math, legal details. |
| **Creative & Language-based:** Editing, communication, tone. | **Real-Time Dependent:** News, current markets, live updates. |
| **Drafting (Starting Points):** Outlines, templates, initial structures. | **Confidential & Private:** Strategy docs, customer PII, trade secrets. |
| **Productivity Shortcuts:** Reformatting, cleaning notes, sorting. | **High-Stakes Decisions:** Medical choices, financial planning, legal guidance. |
| **Human-in-the-Loop:** Tasks where you can review, edit, and verify. | **Structured, Rule-based Processes:** Calculations, spreadsheets, payroll. |

---

## When to Use Gen AI (Suitable Tasks)

Gen AI excels in collaborative, linguistic, and unstructured tasks where exploration and speed are favored over absolute precision.

*   **Exploratory, Not Definitive:** Use AI when seeking a range of options or brainstorming directions, rather than a single source of truth.
    *   *Example:* Brainstorming three campaign ideas.
*   **Creative & Language-Based:** Highly effective for refining communication, adjusting tone, and drafting text.
    *   *Example:* Rewriting a draft email to sound more professional.
*   **Good Starting Points:** Speeds up the initial phases of work by overcoming the "blank page" problem.
    *   *Example:* Drafting an outline for a blog post.
*   **Productivity Shortcuts:** Automates tedious and repetitive operational tasks.
    *   *Example:* Reformatting raw meeting notes into action items.
*   **Human-in-the-Loop Workflows:** Generates base-level assets that will be polished using your own judgment and expertise.
    *   *Example:* Prompting AI to draft a slide deck outline, then modifying and verifying the content yourself.

---

## When to Avoid Gen AI (Unsuitable Tasks)

In situations involving data privacy, high risk, or calculations, traditional software (like databases or calculators) or direct human oversight are required.

> [!WARNING]
> **Do not use Gen AI under these conditions:**

*   **When Accuracy is Critical:** LLMs are predictive engines and can hallucinate false details as facts.
    *   *Example:* Calculating complex tax payments.
*   **When Fresh, Real-Time Information is Needed:** AI models are bound by their training cut-off dates and may serve outdated context.
    *   *Example:* Asking about a law that was passed yesterday.
*   **For Sensitive or Confidential Content:** Sharing internal strategy, business secrets, or Personally Identifiable Information (PII) with public AI tools compromises data security and compliance.
    *   *Example:* Copying internal strategy documents or patient records into a public chatbot.
*   **For High-Stakes Decisions:** Critical decisions require professional human validation and empathy.
    *   *Example:* Deciding whether to change medical treatments or prescriptions.
*   **For Structured, Rule-Based Processes:** Traditional software is deterministic and infinitely more reliable for strict calculations.
    *   *Example:* Processing payroll calculations (where spreadsheets or specialized software should be used).
