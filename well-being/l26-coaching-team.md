# Lesson 26 - Coaching the Team to Support Each Other
*Lesson 26 of 29*

---

## Core Insight: Psychological Safety is a Team Sport

While individual accountability is crucial, maintaining a psychologically safe environment requires collective effort. To build lasting momentum, team members must encourage, coach, and support one another.

Transitioning from individual habits to open, team-wide conversations raises mutual expectations and establishes a self-reinforcing culture of safety and trust.

---

## Coaching for Support: Good vs. Great

Helping the team support one another involves shifting from broad reminders to active peer coaching and feedback.

| Level | Key Behaviors |
| :--- | :--- |
| **What Good Looks Like** | - Promote peer recognition, such as encouraging the team to write a quick, 2-minute daily thank-you note to a colleague.<br>- Remind team members that improving the workplace experience of others is everyone's responsibility. |
| **What Great Looks Like** | - Encourage team members to share diverse perspectives and withhold judgment during idea generation.<br>- Coach team members to support each other (e.g., prompting peers to encourage quieter teammates to speak up).<br>- Actively steer team members away from behaviors that diminish psychological safety (e.g., dismissive remarks, interruption).<br>- Provide direct, constructive feedback and coaching to individuals regarding their personal impact on the team's psychological safety. |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Collective Accountability:** A team's psychological safety is only as strong as its weakest interaction. Steering colleagues away from silencing behaviors and coaching them to support one another ensures that safety remains a shared team norm.
