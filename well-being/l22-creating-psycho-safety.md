## Core Value of Psychological Safety

Psychological safety enables individuals to fully share their unique talents and perspectives. When established, it reduces stress and allows team members to perform at their best, learn continuously, and drive collective performance. 

Creating a psychologically safe space depends on the intentional daily actions of every team member, building upon a foundation of mutual awareness and strong relationships.

---

## The REACH Model for Psychological Safety

![The REACH Model](/assets/the-reach-model-for-psychological-safety.png)

The **REACH** model provides a structured framework of four core actions to help teammates feel comfortable speaking up and taking risks without fear of negative consequences:

| Action | Core Principle | Practical Implementation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **R** - Reframe Mistakes | Treat errors as learning opportunities. | Shift the perspective on mistakes, using them as essential feedback and teaching moments to guide future success. |
| **E** - Encourage All Voices | Actively invite diverse perspectives. | Encourage open dialogue and solicit ideas from all team members to inform decisions and avoid echo chambers. |
| **A** - Appreciate Contributions | Validate input and effort. | Actively acknowledge, value, and support team members when they share ideas or speak up. |
| **CH** - Coach to Help | Foster mutual support structures. | Coach, mentor, and role model supportive behaviors to establish a culture where team members actively help one another. |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Action-Driven Safety:** Psychological safety is not a passive state; it is actively constructed through how we react to mistakes, invite feedback, and appreciate our colleagues every day.
