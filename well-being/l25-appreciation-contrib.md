*Lesson 25 of 29*

## Core Insight: Value the Process, Not Just the Outcome

An attitude of gratitude is essential for cultivating a psychologically safe team environment. However, effective appreciation must extend beyond celebrating final milestones or polished ideas. 

Teams must actively value the behaviors that drive the collaborative process:
*   Admitting mistakes and sharing lessons learned.
*   Initiating difficult conversations and asking hard questions.
*   Vulnerably seeking help when facing challenges.

When team members feel their process-oriented contributions are recognized, they are far more likely to remain engaged and communicative.

---

## Appreciating Contributions: Good vs. Great

Shifting from basic praise to active, process-focused recognition builds deeper trust within teams.

| Level | Key Behaviors |
| :--- | :--- |
| **What Good Looks Like** | - Proactively and frequently acknowledge when team members do a good job.<br>- Say "thank you" when colleagues speak up or go above and beyond. |
| **What Great Looks Like** | - Integrate recognition into the team's daily language and operational norms.<br>- Give special recognition to individuals who raise uncomfortable, difficult issues.<br>- Be specific when celebrating actions, detailing the exact behavior and the personal impact it had on you.<br>- Acknowledge and build on others' inputs in real-time meetings (e.g., *"Building on Cara's point, we could..."*). |

## The Science of Gratitude

Practicing gratitude has documented physical and mental health benefits:

> [!TIP]
> **The Gratitude Advantage:** Research shows that keeping a gratitude journal increases average happiness by **25 percent**. Additionally, individuals who actively practice gratitude experience the following health outcomes:
> *   **Better Sleep:** An average of 30 additional minutes of sleep each night.
> *   **More Activity:** 33 percent more physical exercise per week.
> *   **Cardiovascular Health:** Up to a 10 percent reduction in systolic blood pressure.
> *   **Healthier Habits:** Up to a 20 percent decrease in dietary fat intake.
> *   **Longevity:** A potential increase in life expectancy of up to 7 years.
