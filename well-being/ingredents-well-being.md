# The McKinsey Well-being Framework: A Holistic Approach to Performance

This framework outlines the essential ingredients for maintaining high performance and personal resilience. Your well-being "battery" is sustained by four distinct energy sources. True mastery of well-being requires a balanced integration of all four, rather than over-prioritizing just one or two.

---

## I. The Four Sources of Well-being

### 1. Physical Energy

_The foundation of performance, focusing on biological recovery and vitality._

#### **Sleep**

- **Individual Habits:**
  - **Consistency:** Set a daily cutoff for work/screens and maintain a regular sleep schedule.
  - **Quantity:** Aim for 7 to 8 hours of restorative sleep.
  - **Ritual:** Establish a "power down" ritual (e.g., light reading) to signal the end of the day.
- **Team Habits:**
  - **Boundaries:** Commit to "no-contact" windows (early mornings, evenings, and weekends).

#### **Exercise**

- **Individual Habits:**
  - **Consistency:** Maintain or escalate weekly fitness goals.
  - **Micro-movements:** Leverage spontaneous gaps (e.g., meeting cancellations) to move.
  - **Recovery:** Take regular stretching breaks throughout the day.
- **Team Habits:**
  - **Dynamic Meetings:** Schedule "audio-only" or "mobile" meetings where video/laptops aren't required, allowing movement.
  - **Accountability:** Discuss and support personal fitness goals during weekly check-ins.

#### **Nutrition**

- **Individual Habits:**
  - **Incremental Change:** Start with small, manageable goals (e.g., eliminating sugary drinks).
  - **Self-Regulation:** Adjust food intake based on daily activity levels.
  - **Hydration:** Maintain optimal water intake.
- **Team Habits:**
  - **Rest Protection:** Block shared lunch times on calendars where meetings are prohibited.

---

### 2. Mental & Emotional Energy

_The capacity for focus, creativity, and emotional regulation._

#### **Hobbies**

- **Individual Habits:** Proactively block recurring time for solo or group hobbies.
- **Team Habits:** Use check-ins to hold each other accountable for pursuing personal passions.

#### **Meditation & Mindfulness**

- **Individual Habits:** Practice daily meditation, even in small increments, to build long-term resilience.
- **Team Habits:** Offer short, optional team-wide meditation sessions daily.

#### **Focus & Flow**

- **Individual Habits:**
  - Practice single-tasking.
  - Eliminate cognitive "leaks": put phones away and disable email notifications during deep work.
- **Team Habits:** Establish collective norms (e.g., "no-email" zones during meetings).

#### **Introspection**

- **Individual Habits:** Use journaling or reflective practices to process inner thoughts.
- **Team Habits:** Schedule monthly "Team Reflection" sessions to discuss purpose, meaning, and challenges.

---

### 3. Social Energy

_The strength of our connections and our sense of belonging._

#### **Relationships & Societal Role**

- **Individual Habits:**
  - **Alignment:** Define personal values and ensure priorities match your purpose.
  - **Engagement:** Get involved in local community initiatives.
  - **Resilience:** During energy lows, reconnect with your "Why" to regain perspective.
- **Team Habits:**
  - **Shared Vision:** Facilitate explicit conversations about the team's values and mission.
  - **Collective Impact:** Participate in team volunteering or cause-based support.

---

### 4. Spiritual Energy

_Connection to a higher purpose, professional calling, and gratitude._

#### **Professional Calling**

- **Individual Habits:** Regularly reflect on how your specific work contributions connect to a larger purpose.
- **Team Habits:** Analyze the team's impact collectively—understanding what the work means to individuals, the team, and the organization.

#### **Spiritual Connection & Gratitude**

- **Individual Habits:**
  - Start the day with positive affirmations.
  - Practice active gratitude for small daily wins.
- **Team Habits:** Embed gratitude into meeting structures (e.g., starting or ending with affirmations or celebrations).

---

## II. Principles for Implementation

To maximize the impact of these habits, apply the following four core principles:

1.  **Be Specific & Focused:** Don't attempt everything at once. Pick precise actions that address your biggest energy gap.
2.  **Continuous Re-energizing:** Energy management is an all-day task. Use micro-moments throughout the day to recharge rather than waiting for a single "event."
3.  **Harness Cues:** Identify existing situational triggers (e.g., a specific time of day or a recurring meeting) that can prompt healthy behaviors.
4.  **Leverage Community:** Find "accountability partners" to share ideas, offer feedback, and provide mutual support.
