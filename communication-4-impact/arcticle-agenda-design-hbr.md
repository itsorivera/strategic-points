# Strategic Agenda Design for Effective Meetings

_Based on insights by Roger Schwarz (Harvard Business Review)._

An effective agenda is the foundation of a productive meeting. It sets clear expectations, optimizes time, and ensures the team remains focused on high-impact outcomes.

## Core Principles of Agenda Design

### 1. Structure Topics as Questions

Instead of using vague phrases (e.g., "Budget Review"), frame topics as specific questions (e.g., "What criteria will we use to reallocate the Q3 budget?"). This helps participants prepare and clearly identifies when a discussion is complete.

### 2. Define the Interaction Type

Clearly state the purpose of each item to manage expectations:

- **Share Information**: Purely for updates (ideally sent as pre-reads).
- **Seek Input**: Gathering diverse perspectives for a decision to be made later.
- **Make a Decision**: Reaching a final agreement (specify the decision-making rule up front).

### 3. Prioritize Interdependent Issues

Focus team time on topics that affect everyone present and requires the whole group to solve. Avoid using collective time for items that only impact a subset of the team.

---

## Tactical Execution

- **Seek Team Input**: Build engagement by including items that reflect the team's needs.
- **Realistic Time Allocation**: Calculate time based on the number of participants and the depth of the required consensus.
- **Propose a Process**: Define the steps (e.g., "10m info sharing, 10m ideation, 15m decision") so the team moves together rather than in parallel.
- **Assigned Leadership**: Clearly identify who is responsible for leading each segment.
- **Pre-Meeting Preparation**: Explicitly state what participants must read or do before arriving.

## Continuous Improvement: The Plus/Delta

End every meeting with a quick 5-minute review:

- **Plus**: What did we do well today?
- **Delta**: What should we do differently next time?
