# Purpose: Strategic Alignment in Communication

Purpose is the second pillar of the EPIC framework. It involves defining the intended outcome of an interaction by strategically balancing your objectives with the needs of your audience.

## The Multifaceted Definition of Purpose

Defining a purpose is more than just stating what you want. It requires an inclusive approach that transforms a one-way transmission into a meaningful dialogue:

1.  **Feasibility (Empathetic Context)**: Given the audience's current mindset and feelings, what is **realistically achievable** in this specific interaction?
2.  **Internal Objectives**: What are your clear goals and desired outcomes for this meeting?
3.  **Audience Objectives**: What does the other person seek to gain from the interaction?

## Key Strategic Questions

To ensure your communication is purposeful and impactful, evaluate every interaction through these three lenses:

- **Audience Needs**: What do they want to get out of this interaction?
- **Personal Needs**: What do you intend to achieve?
- **The Greater Goal**: How does this specific interaction serve the broader project or long-term relationship?

---

> [!TIP]
> **Crystal Clarity**: Moving from a general "update" to a multifaceted purpose ensures your communication is inclusive, builds stronger relationships, and reaches objectives more effectively.
