# Developing a Compelling Storyline

_Key Insights from McKinsey Forward Program - Lesson 33_

## From Logic to Narrative

Once an airtight logical structure has been established using the Pyramid Principle, the next step is to translate that structure into a storyline. A storyline acts as the bridge between raw data analysis and the narrative materials (presentations, emails, or speeches) used to influence colleagues and decision-makers.

### Crucial Distinction: Storylining vs. Storytelling

While often used interchangeably, these represent two high-level stages of preparation:

1.  **Storylining:** The process of converting the Pyramid Principle and synthesis into a prose-based outline. Often called a "dot-dash," this is the logical skeleton that determines the order of key points and the placement of supporting evidence. It is primarily concerned with clarity and logical flow.
2.  **Storytelling:** The creative layer applied on top of the storyline. It focuses on how to engage, hook, and inspire the audience. It uses emotional angles, anecdotes, and creative delivery to make the logical argument resonate.

---

## The Strategic Five-Step Formula

To move an audience to action, a storyline should follow a targeted narrative sequence rather than presenting a comprehensive list of all findings.

1.  **Introduction (Situation and Complication):** Briefly describe the current context and the specific problem or challenge being addressed.
2.  **The Question:** Formally raise the core question that the presentation or interaction aims to answer.
3.  **Primary Recommendation:** Lead with the solution, directly drawn from the governing thought of your pyramid.
4.  **Support and Expansion:** Provide the necessary arguments and data (foundational facts) to justify the recommendation.
5.  **Conclusion:** Summarize the key points and reiterate the path forward.

---

## Strategic Selection of Insights

A common pitfall is the temptation to share the full range of insights gathered during the research phase. High-impact communication requires the discipline to step back and select only the targeted set of insights necessary to drive a specific action.

- **Objective:** Every element of the storyline must serve the meeting's specific purpose.
- **Result:** A narrative that is focused, persuasive, and time-efficient.
