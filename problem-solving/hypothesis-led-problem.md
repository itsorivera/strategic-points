# Hypothesis-Led Problem Solving

The hypothesis-led approach is a structured framework for solving complex problems through convergent thinking, iteration, and continuous communication. It moves beyond raw data collection by focusing efforts on validating specific, high-impact assumptions.

## The 7-Step Framework

![alt text](/problem-solving/assets/hypothesis-led-problem.png)
_The hypothesis led approach to problem solving by McKinsey & Company_

### 1. Define the Problem

Focus on **impact**. Clearly articulate the core challenge and identify the critical knowledge required to move forward.

### 2. Structure the Problem

Deconstruct the problem into manageable components. Formulate **early hypotheses** regarding the key drivers and underlying causes.

### 3. Prioritize Issues

Maximize **speed and efficiency**. Filter the decomposed elements to focus exclusively on the issues most critical to the final outcome.

### 4. Develop a Workplan

Optimize **resource allocation**. Design a targeted analysis plan that determines exactly where and how time should be spent to yield the highest ROI.

### 5. Conduct Analyses

Gather **evidence**. Systematically test the prioritized hypotheses to either prove or disprove them with objective data.

### 6. Synthesize Findings

Focus on the **"So What?"**. Move beyond observations to extract the deeper implications and strategic patterns revealed by the data.

### 7. Develop Recommendations

Define the **solution**. Transform synthesized insights into specific, actionable recommendations tailored to solve the original problem.
